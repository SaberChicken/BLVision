Frequently Asked Questions

We understand that there can be a lot of questions when it comes to contact lenses and eye care.
That's why we've compiled a list of frequently asked questions to provide you with the answers and information you need. From questions about the types of lenses we offer to tips on caring for your contacts, we've got you covered.

If you can't find the answer you're looking for, please don't hesitate to contact us directly. Our knowledgeable team is here to provide you with the personalized care and support you deserve.
What types of contact lenses do you offer?
We offer a wide variety of contact lenses to suit different needs, including daily disposable lenses, monthly disposable lenses, toric lenses for astigmatism, and multifocal lenses for presbyopia.
How often should I replace my contact lenses?
It is generally recommended to replace your contact lenses on a regular basis to maintain their quality and minimize the risk of infection. The replacement schedule will depend on the type of lenses you have and your individual needs, but we can provide you with personalized recommendations during your appointment.
Do you offer daily disposable contact lenses?
Yes, we offer daily disposable contact lenses for those who prefer the convenience and hygiene of single-use lenses.
Can I wear contact lenses if I have astigmatism?
Yes, we offer toric contact lenses specifically designed for people with astigmatism.
What should I do if my contact lens tears or becomes damaged?
If your contact lens tears or becomes damaged, remove it immediately and dispose of it properly. It is important to avoid wearing damaged lenses as they can cause irritation and potential infection.
How do I care for my contact lenses?
Proper care and maintenance are essential for healthy and comfortable contact lens wear. We can provide you with detailed instructions on how to clean, disinfect, and store your lenses, as well as tips on avoiding common mistakes and preventing eye infections.
Can I sleep in my contact lenses?
This will depend on the type of contact lenses you have and your individual needs. Generally, it is not recommended to sleep in your contact lenses as it can increase the risk of eye infections and other complications.
What should I do if my contact lenses are causing discomfort?
If your contact lenses are causing discomfort, it is important to remove them and contact us for an evaluation. Discomfort can be a sign of an underlying issue, such as dry eye or an ill-fitting lens.
Do you accept insurance for contact lens purchases?
We accept a variety of insurance plans for contact lens purchases. Please contact us for more information and to verify your coverage.
How often should I schedule an eye exam?
It is generally recommended to schedule an eye exam at least once a year to monitor your eye health and update your prescription as needed. However, your individual needs may vary based on your age, medical history, and other factors. We can provide you with personalized recommendations during your appointment.
